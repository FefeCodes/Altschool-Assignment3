Nigerian Party Jollof Rice

Nigerian Jollof Rice is a vibrant, flavorful dish made with rice, tomatoes, peppers, and aromatic spices.

Recipe Information

Prep time: 20 minutes

Cook time: 1 hour 10 minutes

Servings: 6

Difficulty: Intermediate

Ingredients

  • 4 cups long-grain parboiled rice
  • 5 large tomatoes
  • 4 red bell peppers (tatashe)
  • 2 medium onions (1 for blending, 1 for frying)
  • 3 Scotch bonnet peppers (atarodo)
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 4 cups chicken stock
  • 2 teaspoons curry powder
  • 2 teaspoons thyme
  • 2 bay leaves
  • Salt to taste
  • Seasoning cubes (Maggi or Knorr)

Instructions

  1. Rinse rice in cold water until the water runs clear, then set aside.
  2. Blend tomatoes, bell peppers, Scotch bonnet peppers, and one onion until smooth. Pour into a pot and boil on medium heat for 10-15 minutes to reduce excess water.
  3. In a large pot, heat vegetable oil. Add sliced onions and sauté until fragrant.
  4. Stir in tomato paste and fry for 3-5 minutes to remove its raw taste.
  5. Add the boiled pepper mixture and fry on medium heat for 15 minutes, stirring occasionally to prevent burning.
  6. Season with curry powder, thyme, bay leaves, seasoning cubes, and salt. Stir well.
  7. Pour in chicken stock and allow the sauce to simmer for 5 minutes.
  8. Add washed rice into the pot and stir until the grains are well coated with sauce.
  9. Cover the pot with foil or parchment paper before placing the lid to trap the steam. Reduce heat to low.
  10. Cook for 30-35 minutes, checking halfway to stir gently and ensure it’s not burning.
  11. Once rice is soft and liquid has absorbed, remove from heat. Let it sit covered for 5 minutes before serving.
  12. Fluff rice with a fork and enjoy your smoky, flavorful Jollof Rice!

Cooking Tips

For an authentic, party-style smoky flavor, let the bottom of the rice toast slightly without burning, the crispy layer is called “party rice” and it’s a favorite!
